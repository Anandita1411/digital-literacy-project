## Project Overview
This repository presents my Digital Literacy Portfolio developed as part of the Digital Literacy course at Vityarthi. The purpose of this project is to build and demonstrate essential digital skills required for academic and professional growth in today’s technology-driven world. Through a series of practical tasks, this portfolio reflects my understanding of how to use digital tools effectively, communicate responsibly online, and stay aware of potential cyber threats.

---

## Project Description
The project is structured into five tasks, each focusing on a different aspect of digital literacy. The first task involves creating an awareness infographic to explain the concept and importance of digital literacy in a visually engaging manner. The second task focuses on building a professional digital presence by setting up and organizing profiles on platforms such as GitHub and LinkedIn.

The third task explores coding and collaboration platforms, where I practiced problem-solving and created a Google Form to spread awareness among peers. The fourth task emphasizes professional communication by drafting formal emails and preparing a social media etiquette checklist. The final task involves researching cybercrime, understanding its impact, and creating a prevention guide to promote safe online practices.

---

## Tools and Platforms Used
Throughout this project, I used various digital tools and platforms such as Canva for designing the infographic, GitHub for repository management, LinkedIn for professional networking, HackerRank or CodeChef for coding practice, and Google Forms for creating an interactive awareness quiz. These tools helped me gain hands-on experience in different areas of digital literacy.

---

## Learning Outcome
This project has helped me develop a deeper understanding of responsible digital behavior, effective communication, and online safety. It has also improved my ability to use modern digital platforms confidently and professionally. Overall, this portfolio reflects my growth as a digitally aware and responsible student.
